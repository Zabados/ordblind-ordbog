# Ordblind Ordbog — Dansk–Engelsk og Engelsk–Dansk

A dyslexia-friendly Danish–English and English–Danish dictionary, built for the PD3 written exam (B2–C1 vocabulary).

---

## The idea

Most bilingual dictionaries assume you can skim a page at speed. For readers with dyslexia that is often the hardest part — by the time you have found the word, the sentence you were holding in your head is gone.

This dictionary is designed around that problem:

- **Consistent colour coding** — each part of speech and gender has its own colour, so you recognise the word type before you read the label
- **Phonetic guide in Danish** — pronunciation written out in Danish sounds (e.g. *BEHRE*, *TOH-LER*), so you do not need to know IPA to check how a word sounds
- **Example sentences** — real sentences from Tatoeba and Leipzig Corpora, showing the word in everyday use
- **Alphabet strip on the edge** — every page shows the full A–Å (or A–Z) alphabet on the spine edge with the current section highlighted, so you can find your place without counting pages
- **A4 print layout** — designed to be printed and bound, not read on a screen

The word list is drawn from frequency data (Leipzig Corpora, 1 million Danish news words) and PD3 exam vocabulary.

---

## Status

The dictionary is a work in progress. The entry pipeline is automated for grammar, pronunciation, and example sentences; editorial review of translations is ongoing.

We are planning to self-publish a printed edition. When that happens, ordering information will be added here so anyone who wants a physical copy can get one at cost — this will never be a commercial product.
